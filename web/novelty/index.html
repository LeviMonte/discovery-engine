How this demo flags "novelty" (out-of-distribution)

Superconductor tab — range check. If any input descriptor leaves the min–max seen in training, the prediction is flagged as extrapolation. Simple and transparent.

Molecule tab — epistemic uncertainty. When the 5 ensemble members disagree more than they did on 90% of training molecules, the compound is flagged as novel/uncertain — a model-driven novelty signal that needs no lookup table.

Why epistemic, not just distance? Epistemic variance (disagreement across the ensemble) is exactly the "have I seen things like this?" term. It shrinks with more data and grows in unexplored regions — the principled quantity for deciding what to measure next, which is the whole thesis of the discovery benchmark.
