Does calibrated uncertainty make materials discovery more sample-efficient?

An interactive companion to a retrospective benchmarking study across two very different chemistries — inorganic superconductors and organic molecules. You can poke the trained models live and replay the actual active-learning experiments. This is a methods demo, not a claim to have discovered a new material.

🎛️

Predict & explain →

Adjust a composition, or draw a molecule and get live μ ± σ back (real in-browser inference for both domains), with the aleatoric/epistemic split and an out-of-distribution warning.
🧬

Generate candidates →

Inverse design: an evolutionary search mutates molecules, scores each with the live ensemble, and surfaces the best — ranked by predicted property, uncertainty, and synthesizability (SAscore). The "propose new things" half of discovery, honestly framed as leads to investigate.
📈

Discovery replay →

Overlay six acquisition strategies and watch the real saved discovery curves grow measurement-by-measurement, with 95% CI bands over 5 seeds and live enrichment / AUDC metrics.
🎯

Confidence & novelty →

The real calibration diagnostics: is the ± band honest, does σ rank error, and how novelty (out-of-distribution) gets flagged. Knowing what you don't know — the core of predicting the unknown.
🏆

Leaderboard →

Your predictions auto-save locally and rank against real benchmark reference materials. Click any entry for honest, referenced synthesis context — and submit your best find via GitHub.

The three findings, honestly

1 · Sample efficiency. Model-guided search finds the top 5% of materials ~5× (superconductors) / ~3× (solubility) faster than random — but adding an uncertainty bonus (UCB/Thompson/EI) does not beat plain greedy. Calibrated model means do the work.

2 · Calibration. Against a fair single network the deep ensemble does not improve raw calibration; it slightly over-disperses and needs post-hoc recalibration. Reported plainly, not hidden.

3 · Transfer. The same machinery — changed only at the featurizer — gives the same strategy ranking in both chemistries.
